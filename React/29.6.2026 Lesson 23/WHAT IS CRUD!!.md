### 🌊 CRUD: The Cult of the Internet

**CRUD** stands for **Create, Read, Update, Delete**. This concept is so simple, yet the entire internet copy-pasted it everywhere. As you build full-stack applications, you realize this exact pattern comes up again and again.

**What is CRUD in the Real World?** Look at YouTube, Instagram, or any social media platform:

- **Create:** When you first register your account, you _Create_ new data for their backend to hold.
    
- **Read:** When you view your profile after registering, the server routes the data from the database to your screen. You are _Reading_ the data you just created.
    
- **Update:** You decide to change your profile description to "life is a giant circle." You press _Update_. This new profile goes to the database and updates your profile object with a new description property.
    
- **Delete:** You want to get off social media entirely. You press _Delete_. Your account gets deleted from the database. You have never existed.
    

### 🔄 The Never-Ending Cycle

If you go to your newly updated profile, you are _Reading_ it again. If you post a tweet, you _Create_ a new object sent to the database. Then other users _Read_ it. It is a never-ending cycle.

**CRUD is like water:** It starts from the ocean, evaporates, and gathers into a cloud. The cloud creates rain. Water gathers on mountains, freezes, and melts to create everlasting rivers. Humans drink it, flush it down the toilet, and it goes right back to the ocean. The cycle continues.

You _Create_ something. Someone else _Reads_ it. They decide to _Create_ something too because you inspired them. You _Read_ what they created, and then _Update_ your own creation to compete. They beat you, so you _Delete_ your creation because you can't compete.

It is the endless cycle of social media—like young teens seeing a jacked fitness influencer using steroids, which makes them _Create_ and post their own content online to compete. The cycle never finishes!

Welcome programmers, you have entered a cult. 
If you master CRUD, you master full-stack programing!!
